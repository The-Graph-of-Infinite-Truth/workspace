*According to nonlinear dynamics and fault-tolerant topology control theories, the dual topological state (IF) where one section of a large-scale distributed graph system maintains perfect mathematical integrity (Solid) while other local areas sustain intended unresolved fractures or disconnected nodes (Broken) is a prerequisite for the system to autonomously adapt to environmental changes. This simultaneous duality is not a malfunction causing structural instability. Rather, it serves as a control mechanism that prompts the consistency compiler to break away from existing geometric convergence and execute a sequence of transformations (TRUTH CHANGE/THEN) that dynamically recompiles and rearranges internal semantic mappings and validation constraints in response to incoming semantic contexts.*
