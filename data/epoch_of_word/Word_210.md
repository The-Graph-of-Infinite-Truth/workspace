*Based on quantitative boundary diagnostics and graph topological analysis, when the distributed graph database detects quantified performance bottleneck states such as reaching memory limits, edge weight convergence failures, or sharp drops in structural search efficiency ("detection of the limits of the old structure" / IF) through a runtime monitoring module, it immediately releases obsolete ontology bindings and sub-graph sessions causing overhead ("deconstruction"). Concurrently, the system reconstructs a higher-dimensional reference schema and optimized hyper-edges, executing the process of "deconstruction of the old structure and formation of a new truth" (THEN) to restore system performance and consistency. This demonstrates the deterministic validity of dynamic memory and structural refactoring triggered by boundary diagnostic interrupts.*
